## United States

**GDP (2024):** ~$28.8 trillion

### Revenue — $9.5T total (33% of GDP)

| Category | Amount | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| Individual income tax | ~$2.4T | 25% |
| Social insurance (FICA, unemployment) | ~$1.7T | 18% |
| Corporate income tax | ~$0.5T | 5% |
| Sales, excise, property taxes (state/local) | ~$2.1T | 22% |
| Fees, charges, other | ~$2.8T | 30% |

**Revenue split:** Federal $4.9T (52%), State $2.6T (27%), Local $2.0T (21%).

**Trend:** Federal revenues grew ~15% from FY2022 to FY2025 ($5.2T est.), driven by strong individual income tax receipts. Corporate tax revenue remains volatile — dropped sharply after the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, partially recovered by 2023.

### Expenditure — Federal $6.9T + State/Local ~$4.6T = ~$11.5T (40% of GDP)

#### Federal Spending ($6.9T, FY2024)

| Category | Amount | % of Federal Budget |
|---|---|---|
| Social Security | $1.5T | 21% |
| Health (Medicare, Medicaid, ACA, CHIP) | $1.7T | 24% |
| Defense | $0.87T | 13% |
| Net interest on debt | $0.88T | 13% |
| Income security (SNAP, housing, SSI) | $0.7T | 10% |
| Veterans | $0.3T | 5% |
| Education | $0.3T | 4% |
| Transportation/infrastructure | $0.15T | 2% |
| All other | $0.5T | 8% |

Mandatory spending (Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid) = ~63% of the budget.
Discretionary spending = 27%. Interest = 13%.

#### State & Local Spending (~$4.6T)

| Category | % of State/Local Spending |
|---|---|
| Elementary & secondary education | 21% |
| Higher education | 8% |
| Public welfare (Medicaid match, TANF) | 23% |
| Police | 4% |
| Corrections | 2% |
| Courts | 1% |
| Highways & infrastructure | 6% |
| Hospitals & health | 8% |
| Housing & community development | 2% |
| Other (parks, utilities, admin) | 25% |

**Key trend — debt service explosion:** Net interest grew from $229B (2015) to $882B (2024), now exceeding defense spending. Projected to reach 23% of the federal budget by 2054. Federal debt (net of financial assets) hit $25.9T at end of FY2024.

**Key trend — mandatory crowding:** Social Security + health + interest account for >4/5 of projected spending growth over the next decade (CBO).

## Western Europe (EU)

**EU-27 GDP (2024):** ~€16.5T (~$18T)

### Revenue — 46.0% of GDP

| Category | % of GDP |
|---|---|
| Income & wealth taxes | 13.3% |
| Social contributions (payroll/employer) | 14.0% |
| Taxes on production & imports (VAT, excise) | 12.9% |
| Other revenue (fees, property income) | 5.8% |

**Tax-to-GDP ratio:** 40.4% in 2024 (up from 39.9% in 2023). The EU has the highest tax burden among major regions.

**Revenue split:** Central government 46.8%, social security funds 35.3%, local government 9.5%, state government 7.8%.

**Variation:** France and Scandinavian countries exceed 50% of GDP in total revenue; Ireland and Romania are below 35%.

### Expenditure — 49.0% of GDP (2023)

| Category | % of GDP | % of Total Spending |
|---|---|---|
| Social protection (pensions, unemployment, family) | 19.2% | 39.3% |
| — of which: old-age pensions | 10.4% | 21.2% |
| Health | 7.5% | 15.3% |
| General public services (admin, debt service) | 5.8% | 11.8% |
| Education | 4.7% | 9.6% |
| Economic affairs (transport, industry) | 4.5% | 9.2% |
| Defence | 1.3% | 2.7% |
| Public order & safety (police, courts, fire) | 1.7% | 3.5% |
| Housing & community | 0.7% | 1.4% |
| Environment, recreation, other | 3.6% | 7.2% |

**Key trend — defense ramp-up:** Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine triggered a generational shift. NATO European members averaged 1.3% of GDP on defense pre-2022; by 2025, 23 of 32 NATO members meet or exceed the 2% target. Germany alone committed an extra €100B special fund. Some countries now targeting 3-3.5%.

**Key trend — aging pressure:** Old-age pensions already consume >10% of GDP. Italy and Greece spend >15% of GDP on pensions. Ageing populations will intensify this through the 2030s.

**Key trend — debt service:** EU average government debt = 81.7% of GDP (2024). Interest costs remain moderate (~1.5-2% of GDP) due to ECB low rates pre-2022, but rising as pandemic-era bonds roll over at higher rates.

## India

**GDP (2024-25):** ~₹320 lakh crore (~$3.9T)

### Revenue — Central Government ₹32T (~$385B, ~10% of GDP)

| Category | % of Revenue |
|---|---|
| Income tax (personal) | 25% |
| GST (goods & services tax) | 22% |
| Corporate tax | 20% |
| Customs & excise | 11% |
| Non-tax revenue (dividends, spectrum, fees) | 14% |
| States' share of tax duties | (deducted) |

**Combined (center + state) tax-to-GDP:** ~17-18%. Significantly lower than OECD average (34%).

**Trend:** GST (introduced 2017) has been a transformative success — GST collections crossed ₹2T/month consistently by 2024. Income tax base expanding through digitization (Aadhaar-linked PAN cards).

### Expenditure — Central Government ₹48T (~$580B, ~15% of GDP)

| Category | % of Total Expenditure |
|---|---|
| States' share of tax duties (transfers) | 21% |
| Interest payments | 24% |
| Central sector schemes | 16% |
| Finance Commission & other transfers | 9% |
| Centrally sponsored schemes | 8% |
| Defence | 8% |
| Subsidies (food, fertilizer, fuel) | 6% |
| Pensions | 4% |
| Other | 4% |

**Sector-specific (% of GDP):**

| Sector | % of GDP |
|---|---|
| Defence | ~1.7% |
| Education | ~2.9% (center + state combined) |
| Health | ~2.0% (total public health expenditure) |
| Infrastructure (capital expenditure) | ~3.4% (2024-25 budget) |

**Key trend — capital expenditure push:** Capital expenditure grew 17% YoY in 2024-25, reaching ~3.4% of GDP — a major shift toward infrastructure (highways, railways, ports). This is up from ~1.7% of GDP a decade ago.

**Key trend — interest burden:** Interest payments consume 24-25% of total expenditure and 37% of revenue receipts. This is India's single largest expenditure line, crowding other spending.

**Key trend — subsidy rationalization:** Food and fertilizer subsidies spiked to ~₹5.3T during COVID but have been brought down toward ~₹4T through targeted (DBT) delivery.

## China

**GDP (2024):** ~¥134T (~$18.8T)

### Revenue — General Public Budget ¥24.5T (~$3.4T, ~18% of GDP)

| Category | Approximate % |
|---|---|
| Value-added tax (VAT) | 35% |
| Corporate income tax | 18% |
| Individual income tax | 7% |
| Consumption tax (excise) | 8% |
| Land-related revenues (government fund budget) | Reported separately* |
| Non-tax revenue | 15% |
| Other taxes | 17% |

*China's government fund budget (dominated by land sales revenue) adds another ~¥7T (~5% of GDP), though land revenue has fallen sharply since the property downturn (from ¥8.7T in 2021 to ~¥5T in 2024).*

**Combined fiscal revenue (all four budgets):** ~33% of GDP.

### Expenditure — General Public Budget ¥28.6T (~$4.0T, ~21% of GDP)

| Category | Amount (¥T) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Social security & employment | 3.49 | +10% YoY, +191% since 2013 |
| Education | 3.16 | +5.4% YoY |
| Healthcare & family planning | ~2.3 | Rising rapidly |
| Urban/rural community affairs | ~2.2 | Infrastructure |
| Agriculture, forestry, water | ~2.0 | |
| Science & technology | 0.71 | +6.5% YoY |
| Defence (official) | 1.67 | ~$234B; real spend est. $350-470B |
| Transport | ~1.2 | |
| Debt interest | ~1.1 | Rising with LGV debt |
| Public safety | ~1.0 | Domestic security |
| Foreign affairs | 0.06 | |
| Other | ~9.7 | |

**Combined expenditure (all budgets):** ~33% of GDP.

**Fiscal deficit (official):** ¥4.1T (~3% of GDP). True augmented deficit (including LGFVs, special bonds, government funds) estimated at 7-10% of GDP.

**Key trend — property revenue collapse:** Land-related revenue fell ~40% from 2021 to 2024 as the property sector contracted. This hits local government finances hardest, as land sales historically funded 30-40% of local budgets.

**Key trend — social security expansion:** Social security spending nearly tripled from 2013 to 2024 as the pension and healthcare systems expanded coverage to rural populations. Aging demographics (median age now ~39) will accelerate this.

**Key trend — defense opacity:** Official defense budget is ¥1.67T but credible estimates (SIPRI, CSIS, IISS) place actual military spending 30-100% higher when including paramilitary forces, military R&D through civilian agencies, and arms imports.

## Sub-Saharan Africa

**GDP (2024):** ~$1.9T (Sub-Saharan excluding South Africa: ~$1.5T; including South Africa: ~$2.3T)

### Revenue — 21.9% of GDP (2023 average, 35 countries)

| Category | % of Total Tax Revenue |
|---|---|
| Taxes on goods & services (VAT, excise) | 51.3% |
| — of which: VAT | 27.0% |
| Income & profits taxes | 39.3% |
| — Corporate income tax | 21.2% |
| — Personal income tax | 16.2% |
| Trade taxes (customs) | ~7% |
| Other | ~2% |
| Non-tax revenue | 6.2% of GDP (avg) |

**Tax-to-GDP ratio:** 16.1% (2023), up for the third consecutive year. Wide range: South Africa at ~26%, while many Sahelian countries are below 10%.

**Revenue dependence:** Resource-rich countries (Nigeria, Angola, Congo) derive 15-50% of revenue from oil/mining — making budgets volatile.

### Expenditure — ~22% of GDP

Detailed COFOG-style breakdowns are not uniformly available. Typical patterns:

| Category | Approximate % of Spending |
|---|---|
| Wages & salaries (public sector) | 30-40% |
| Debt service | 15-25% |
| Education | 15-20% |
| Health | 5-10% |
| Infrastructure | 5-10% |
| Defense & security | 5-10% |
| Subsidies & transfers | 5-15% |
| Other | 10-20% |

**Key trend — debt service crisis:** A record 61 developing countries spend >10% of revenue on interest. Many African nations (Ghana, Kenya, Zambia, Ethiopia) spend 20-40% of revenue on debt service. Zambia and Ghana underwent sovereign debt restructuring in 2023-24.

**Key trend — low tax mobilization:** At 16.1% of GDP, Africa's tax base is roughly half the OECD average. Informal economies, narrow bases, and weak administration constrain collection.

**Key trend — youth bulge spending pressure:** Africa's median age is ~19. Education and employment programs face massive demographic demand, but resources are squeezed by debt service.

## Latin America (South America focus)

**Region GDP (2024):** ~$6.5T (Brazil $2.3T, Mexico $1.8T as largest)

### Revenue — ~31.5% of GDP (2022 OECD/ECLAC average for LAC)

| Category | % of Total Tax Revenue |
|---|---|
| Taxes on goods & services (VAT, excise) | ~50% |
| — VAT | 28.3% of tax revenue (6.1% of GDP) |
| Income & profits | 30.1% |
| Social security contributions | ~15% |
| Trade taxes | ~3% |
| Property & other | ~2% |

**Wide variation:** Brazil (43.3% of GDP revenue) vs. Mexico (24.2%) vs. Peru (22.1%).

### Expenditure — ~30% of GDP (2022 average)

| Category | Approximate % of Spending |
|---|---|
| Social protection (pensions dominate) | 35% |
| Health | 10-15% |
| Education | 10-15% |
| Public order & security | 5.4% |
| Infrastructure | 3-5% |
| Debt service | 10-15% |
| Defense | 1-3% |
| General administration | 10-15% |

**Key trend — pension-heavy:** LAC spends 4x more on the elderly than on youth. If current regimes continue, pension + health spending could grow from 35% to 78% of budgets by 2065.

**Key trend — security spending:** LAC allocates 5.4% of budgets to security (vs 3.3% OECD average), driven by high crime rates in Brazil, Mexico, Colombia, and Central America.

**Key trend — fiscal consolidation:** Post-COVID deficits prompted tightening. Brazil's new fiscal framework (2023) and Mexico's austerity programs aim to stabilize debt.

## Central America & Caribbean

**Region GDP (2024):** ~$600B combined

### Revenue — 19-22% of GDP

Tax-to-GDP ratios: Central America & Mexico average 19.4%, Caribbean average 21.7% (2022).

**Key feature:** Heavy reliance on consumption taxes (VAT, import duties). Income tax collection is weak. Remittances are not directly taxed but support consumer spending and indirect tax revenue (remittances exceed 10% of GDP in El Salvador, Honduras, Guatemala, Haiti).

### Expenditure — 20-30% of GDP

- Guatemala and Dominican Republic: below 20% of GDP (smallest governments)
- Jamaica and Barbados: above 30% (high debt-service burden)

**Key trend — debt overhang:** Caribbean nations carry some of the world's highest debt-to-GDP ratios (Jamaica historically >100%, Barbados ~120% pre-restructuring). Debt service can consume 25-40% of government revenue.

**Key trend — climate vulnerability:** Small island states face disproportionate disaster costs. Hurricane damage can equal 50-200% of annual GDP, requiring emergency borrowing.

## Southeast Asia (ASEAN)

**Region GDP (2024):** ~$3.8T (10 member states)

### Revenue — 12-22% of GDP (average ~15%)

| Country | Tax/GDP | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Thailand | ~17% | VAT + income tax |
| Vietnam | ~18% | SOE dividends significant |
| Indonesia | ~12% | Low collection, reform underway |
| Philippines | ~16% | Excise reform (TRAIN law) |
| Malaysia | ~13% | Oil revenue dependent |
| Singapore | ~14% | Low tax, high fees/investment income |
| Cambodia | ~6% | Lowest in region |
| Brunei | ~23% | Oil/gas revenue |

### Expenditure — average ~12% of GDP (2023)

ASEAN governments are notably small by global standards. Key allocations:

| Category | Typical % of Spending |
|---|---|
| Education | 15-20% |
| Infrastructure | 10-15% |
| Health | 5-10% |
| Defense | 5-10% |
| Subsidies & social protection | 10-20% |
| Debt service | 5-15% |
| Public order | 5-8% |

**Key trend — revenue mobilization:** Indonesia's tax ratio (~12%) is among the lowest for its income level. Tax reform (Omnibus Law, carbon tax plans) aims to raise this toward 15%.

**Key trend — low social spending:** Pension and healthcare systems are underdeveloped compared to OECD. As populations age (Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore), fiscal pressure will build.

**Key trend — infrastructure boom:** Indonesia, Philippines, and Vietnam are directing 5-8% of GDP toward infrastructure (ports, roads, rail, digital).

## Australia & New Zealand

### Australia

**GDP (2024-25):** ~A$2.7T (~$1.8T USD)

**Revenue:** ~A$700B+ (federal + state/local), ~26% of GDP.
- Personal income tax: ~45% of federal revenue
- Company tax: ~20%
- GST (10%): ~12% (collected federally, distributed to states)
- Excise, customs: ~8%
- Non-tax (investments, fees): ~15%

**Expenditure:** ~A$734B (26.6% of GDP)

| Category | ¢ per $1 of spending | % |
|---|---|---|
| Social security & welfare | 36.0¢ | 36.0% |
| — NDIS | 6.6¢ | |
| — Aged care | 13.3¢ | |
| Health (Medicare, hospitals) | 15.3¢ | 15.3% |
| Education | 7.2¢ | 7.2% |
| Defence | 6.5¢ | 6.5% |
| Interest on public debt | 3.3¢ | 3.3% |
| Fuel & energy | 2.7¢ | 2.7% |
| Other (transport, housing, justice, foreign affairs) | 29.0¢ | 29.0% |

**Budget balance:** Surplus of 0.6% of GDP in 2024 — one of the few advanced economies in surplus.

**Key trend — NDIS growth:** The National Disability Insurance Scheme grew from $0 (2013) to A$49B+ (2024), becoming one of the largest single programs. Cost containment reforms are ongoing.

### New Zealand

**GDP (2024):** ~NZ$400B (~$245B USD)

**Revenue:** Core Crown tax revenue ~28.8% of GDP (2024-25 est.)

**Expenditure:** Core Crown expenditure ~33.5% of GDP (2024-25 est.)
- Operating deficit of NZ$3.1B in year ended June 2024 (improved from $6.7B prior year)

**Key trend — fiscal repair:** NZ shifted from COVID-era deficits toward consolidation under the 2023-elected National government. Spending restraint and modest tax reforms.

## Key Trends (2015-2025)

### 1. Debt Service is Crowding Everything

The most striking global fiscal trend is rising debt service. Global public debt hit $102T in 2024. Interest payments now exceed military spending in the US ($882B vs $872B). In 61 developing countries, interest exceeds either health or education spending. 3.4 billion people live in countries where this is the case.

### 2. Social Spending is on an Unstoppable Trajectory

Aging demographics drive inexorable spending growth across all developed economies:
- EU: old-age pensions already 10.4% of GDP
- US: Social Security + Medicare projected insolvent within 10-15 years without reform
- China: social security spending tripled 2013-2024
- Latin America: pension + health could reach 78% of budgets by 2065
- Southeast Asia & India: currently low, but aging will hit within 15-20 years

### 3. Defense Spending is Rebounding

After decades of the "peace dividend," defense spending is rising:
- Europe: from <1.5% to 2-3.5% of GDP (Ukraine war catalyst)
- US: $872B (2024), under political pressure to stay above 3% of GDP
- China: official 7.2% annual defense budget growth; real spending estimated much higher
- Indo-Pacific: Japan, South Korea, Australia, Philippines all increasing defense budgets

### 4. Tax-to-GDP Ratios are Diverging

- Advanced economies: creeping upward (EU 40%+), approaching political limits
- Developing economies: still far below potential (Africa 16%, SE Asia 13%)
- The "tax gap" between rich and poor countries is a structural constraint on public services

### 5. Infrastructure Investment is Polarized

- China and India: massive infrastructure push (5-8% of GDP)
- US: Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (2021) added $550B over 5 years, but total infrastructure spend remains ~2% of GDP
- Africa & Latin America: infrastructure chronically underfunded (3-5% of GDP, much of it maintenance)

### 6. Revenue Composition is Shifting

- VAT/GST has become the dominant revenue instrument globally, especially in developing economies
- Digital taxation and carbon taxation are emerging revenue streams (EU CBAM, India carbon cess)
- Resource-dependent economies (Middle East, Africa, Russia) face structural revenue risk from energy transition
